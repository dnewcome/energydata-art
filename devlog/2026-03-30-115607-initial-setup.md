## What happened

Started the project from scratch. The goal is an art visualization workspace around open energy and climate data — CO₂ rise, global energy mix, power plant geography, AI energy loads, carbon markets, whatever stories emerge.

Set up a data fetch pipeline (`scripts/fetch.js`) pulling from no-auth public sources: NOAA Mauna Loa CO₂ (daily + weekly), Our World in Data energy and CO₂ datasets, NASA POWER solar/wind resource API, and the WRI Global Power Plant Database (~35k plants worldwide). EIA and EMBER slots are wired but gated behind free API keys.

Scaffolded an Observable Framework workspace with three starter notebooks:
- **Keeling Curve** — daily CO₂ ppm since 1974, colored by value, with decade averages via DuckDB
- **Global Energy Mix** — stacked area chart of electricity by source, per-country selector, renewables share over time
- **Power Plants** — capacity by fuel type, top countries, filterable table, capacity-vs-year scatter

Data loaders in `src/data/` pipe from `data/raw/` without duplicating large files. Raw data is gitignored; re-fetch anytime with `npm run fetch`.
